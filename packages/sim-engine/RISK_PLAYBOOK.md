## Main attack classes and how to defend

### 1) Toxic informed flow / MEV directional farming

Observed behavior:

- Attackers repeatedly trade with directional edge.
- MM inventory drifts and carry/hedge costs dominate.
- Liquidations do not save the MM because attackers avoid being wrong-side enough.

Defenses:

1. Dynamic toxicity mode (already modeled):
- Trigger on `|truePrice - indexPrice|` and MM drawdown.
- Widen spread, reduce depth, lower leverage, lower OI caps, surcharge fees.

2. Stress mode escalation (already modeled):
- Additional leverage/OI cuts when drawdown exceeds threshold.
- Increase MM hedge-rate while stressed.

3. Production-level fairness controls (still recommended for onchain):
- Frequent batch auctions / call auctions (e.g. 1s cadence) instead of continuous priority.
- Randomized intra-batch ordering or uniform-price clears.
- Minimum quote TTL / no immediate cancel-replace spam lanes.

4. Toxic wallet/account controls (modeled):
- Per-account order-rate and notional-rate limits (now modeled in simulator).
- Per-market order/notional limits and signed imbalance caps (now modeled).
- Projected post-fill initial-margin rejection (spread/impact/fee aware) to block instant under-collateralized opens.
- Optional trader cooldown + respawn flow to test dead-market survivorship bias.
- Toxicity score per account (markout-based), progressively tighter limits (still backlog for production matching).

### 2) Hype -> crash gap and bad debt

Observed behavior:

- Large directional transitions can produce bankruptcies and insurance drain.

Defenses:

1. Volatility-linked leverage:
- Scale max leverage down when recent `|Δlogit|` or sigma rises.

2. Volatility-linked OI:
- Per-market OI cap should shrink with realized volatility and concentration.

3. Insurance-aware caps:
- New OI admitted only if worst-case capped move remains covered by insurance + penalties.

4. Circuit breakers:
- Temporary risk-only mode (close/reduce only) when insurance coverage ratio breaches threshold.

### 3) Entrant manipulation and weak-start exploitation

Observed behavior:

- New entrants can be predictable weak starts, creating one-way flow opportunities.

Defenses:

1. Listing phase (already modeled):
- Lower leverage and OI caps for first 24h.

2. Stabilization ramp (already modeled):
- Increase caps as uncertainty falls / games played rises.

3. Placement calibration:
- Keep high sigma but cap index movement and max leverage during early calibration window.

## Operating policy (recommended)

Use a three-state policy per market: `NORMAL`, `TOXIC`, `STRESS`.

### State entry rules

- Enter `TOXIC` if either:
  - `|truePrice - indexPrice| > toxicityThreshold`, or
  - informed flow share estimate > threshold.

- Enter `STRESS` if either:
  - MM drawdown ratio > stress threshold, or
  - insurance coverage ratio < threshold.

### State actions

- `NORMAL`: standard params.
- `TOXIC`: spread up, depth down, leverage down, OI down, fee surcharge, attack-flow throttle.
- `STRESS`: stronger version of TOXIC + hedge acceleration + optional reduce-only mode.

### Exit rules

- Require hysteresis (e.g. hold state N minutes after trigger clears) to prevent oscillation.

## Risk budgeting and sizing

Minimum invariants:

1. Coverage invariant:
- Worst-case 2-minute capped move loss <= insurance balance + expected liquidation penalties + ADL headroom.

2. Concentration invariant:
- Max single-market MM inventory notional <= X% of MM equity.

3. Drawdown invariant:
- If MM drawdown > Y%, automatically cut leverage and OI, and increase taker fees.
